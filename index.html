What is this page all about?

This is a small website designed to describe the Network of Hollywood celebrities. I have walked on the Hollywood Walk of Fame and acted like a true tourist, lying down on the street and taking pictures with the stars. But are some stars more prestiges than others? Who are the most famous stars? Who is the STAR of stars? This website describes a project the examines the stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

In the project a Network of Hollywood stars will be created and by looking at this more closely some curious things can be said about the celebrities.

The Network

Alright let's explain this "Network" that has been mentioned a couple of times. In the project the network is a mapping of how stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame are linked to each other. The stars are the network's nodes, and these nodes are connected through edges/links.

The list of all stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame can be downloaded from Wikipedia and we can also see what category the stars are in. Now that we have the network's nodes we want to connect them. This is done by looking at the individual Wikipedia pages of the stars and see what other stars they link to. If there is a link from one star to another on the Hollywood Walk of Fame there will be an edge between the nodes. The edges will be directed, meaning they are pointing one way. For example one of the first links on the wikipage of our good friend Samuel L. Jackson is Quentin Tarantino (take a look yourself), because they have collaborated many times in motion pictures. In fact there is also a link from Tarantino's wikipage to Sam Jackson, so in the network there is an edge from Jackson to Tarantino and another edge from Tarantino to Jackson.

The network contains 2296 nodes connected by 25130 so it's exactly what you would call small... The many edges means that some stars are mentioned a lot in wikipages probably because they are big an influential stars. Every node in the network will be a name of a Hollywood celebrity, have the Hollywood star category, and it will also have number as a node attribute. The Hollywood stars have a knack for gaining recognition and fame because of the many award shows they host. The most talented receive awards, this will help tell us who is the STAR of the stars. The node attributes therefore is the number of awards the star has received. An edge can represent many things in this network, friendship, marriage, collaboration, etc., and there are many edges. The most famous and influential star must have appear in many wikipages and so will have many edges in the network.

Properties of the Network

A network has certain characteristics like average degree, degree distribution and connectedness. Further network analysis like detecting and examining communities or centrality measures will be included in the project. For now let's take a look at the basic network properties for the Hollywood star network.

The degree of a node means how many connections it has to other nodes. This tells us something about how connected the network is. It can also give us an idea of what to expect for how many connections famous stars, the average stars and the least famous stars should have. The average degree of the network is 18.605, which means the average star has 18-19 connections to other stars on their wikipages. Our STAR of the stars should there have higher degree than this.

Considering that nodes on average have edges to 18 other nodes means that the network is strongly connected. We are dealing with a giant component where many stars are connected and a fraction is not included. It is possible to move from one node to another in the strongly connected big component using only few steps. This property resembles Six degrees of separation.

Most of the stars are then linked to each other, but what causes the high average degree. Are some stars simply connected to all or is number of connections evenly spread out. The degree distribution will clarify this.

Clearly we can observe that many nodes have in- and out-degrees below the average, and yet there are also nodes with degrees much higher than the average. This does not contradict presence of a giant component, but rather encourages the idea of our STAR of stars. There obviously is stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame that appear on many other Wikipedia pages and have many outgoing links on their own wikipages.

This page has mentioned a couple of times that the most famous and influential stars are the ones with the highest number of links. This is just one measure of centrality in a network called Degree centrality. 4 different centrality measures are examined in the project's network analysis. Community detection

Now it's time to look at the stars and see if they group up in nice ways, kind of like looking at stars in the sky and finding the Big Dipper. In the project the best partition of the network is found with an algorithm designed for exactly this, but it might also be fascinating to know if the network can be divided into groups that we have proposed. We might be able to answer about the Hollywood Walk of Fame network such as... Can we see communities based on the categorisation (actors with actors, singers with singers, etc.)? Is their a group of stars with awards and a group of the more unfortunate stars without? We can look at the modularity to see if communities makes sense. Modularity measure the strength of division of a network into groups.

First up is the best partition which can be found using the Python Louvain algorithm. The groups of stars we get from this is the so-called best partition. It appears the we have some lonely celebrities because the best partition 108 groups and 97 of these are groups of 1. A couple of the groups contain a more reasonable number of stars, 404, 401, 612, 368, 269, 22, 55 and 62. Later we can use text analysis to see what kind of stars are in the different groups. For now let's just mention that the best partition has a modularity of 0.786. We can show the network where the colour of each node indicates what group it is part of.

There definitely groups with more nodes in them, and these groups also seem to part of the giant component.

In order to look for a Categorisation community the categories for each star is needed. Luckily these are included in the Wikipedia list of Hollywood Walk of Fame stars. This is how the stars get partitioned.

  • Motion picture: 1125
  • Broadcast television: 483
  • Audio recording: 420
  • Broadcast radio: 188
  • Theatre/Live performance: 44 This partition has a modularity of 0.570. So even though the stars actually have categories, they can be partitioned better as seen above and thereby get a higher modularity.

It is easy to divide the network into a group of award winning stars and the rest, we can simply look at the node attributes. This is how the stars get partitioned.

  • Awards: 1673
  • No awards: 623 This partition has a modularity of 0.495. Admitted, it gives more sense to test the category partition, but not much needed to be added to test this partition since we already have the node attributes.

Text analysis

Lots of the stuff on this page has been about the network of Hollywood stars. Let's in this section focus celebrities and see if any common information can be found. We can take a look at the text in the Wikipedia pages and use some text analysis to retrieve words that are Hollywood specific. To do this a couple steps are completed. First you download the text of the Wikipedia page, then separate all words in the text and count them. This is where we find the term frequency (TF) of words. Next step is to count "normal" a word is in the text. We want to find important words that are related to the Hollywood Walk of Fame and not just end up with words like "the" and "of". If a word appears in many of the texts we consider it very normal and therefore uninteresting, This is where we find inverse document frequency (IDF) of words. Final step is to combine TF and IDF, now we have assigned values to each word in all Wikipedia pages. The TF-IDF values varies depending on how wikipages we are looking at, so it can be applied to the community detection. But let's take a look at the most Hollywood related words overall.

These words clearly relates to subject of Hollywood stars, as they relates to the entertainment industry "record", "picture", "performance", "studio" "artist", which is exactly what these coral pink stars is given for. "brother" is weirdly enough related to Hollywood, so too bad for you guys with sisters out there, the fame is in the brotherhood. Something as nice as "friend" is apparently relevant here, so as long as you have friend you are star-material, everybody wins.

Data from Wikipedia

There are plenty of information on Hollywood celebrities on Wikipedia. Since the basis for the project is the list of stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame found on Wikipedia it makes sense to also download their individual wikipages while we are at it. We are also looking at awards in order to create node attributes and to use for community detection. The dataset consists of the list stars, their individual Wikipedia pages and lists of winners and nominees of one selected award for each category.
The considered awards are:

  • Academy awards (Film): best actor, best actress, best supporting actor, best supporting actress
  • Primetime Emmy Awards (Television): lead actor comedy, lead actress comedy, lead actor drama, lead actress drama
  • Radio Academy's Hall of Fame (Radio): Members and Fellows
  • Tony Awards (Live performance): leading actor play, leading actress play, leading actor musical, leading actress musical
  • Grammy Awards (Recording): Album of the Year, Record of the Year, Song of the Year, Best New Artist
